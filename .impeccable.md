## Design Context

### Product Reality

Omniscient is an always-on AI assistant for Linux that:
- Watches the user's screen all day
- Hears every conversation
- Knows the user intimately — what they work on, who they talk to, what they care about
- Gets glanced at dozens of times per day
- Runs alongside everything else (terminal, browser, IDE)

This is the most intimate piece of software on the user's machine. The design must respect that.

### Users

A developer/creator working on Linux (currently Salah on Nobara/KDE). Uses the app in long sessions throughout the day, often at night alongside dark terminal environments. Cares about visuals and craft — gets put off by anything that looks like generic SaaS or AI slop.

### Brand Personality (3 words)

**Perceptive · Composed · Alive**

- **Perceptive** — notices things the user would miss
- **Composed** — never urgent, never cluttered, never demanding attention
- **Alive** — subtle motion shows it's active, not a static dashboard

### Emotional Goal

When you open Omniscient, you should feel: *"Everything is captured. Everything is organized. I can trust this."*

Not excitement. Not urgency. **Quiet confidence.**

Like turning to a brilliant assistant who's been sitting beside you all day — they have something useful for you, but they were never demanding your attention.

### The Metaphor: Trusted Observer

Not a dashboard. Not a journal. Not a sci-fi interface.

A trusted observer that's always there. They never interrupt, but they're always aware. When you turn to look at them, they have something useful. Quiet personality — calm but present. They don't show you flashy charts. They show you what matters.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Warm dark.** Not cold void-black. Think a dimly lit study at night, not outer space.

Single signature color: **a warm amber/gold accent** (around `#d4a574` or `oklch(0.78 0.10 75)`) — like a candle or a single lamp in the room. Used sparingly. When it appears, it means something — never decorative.

Typography drives the experience. A distinctive display font for headings (NOT Inter, NOT Space Grotesk). Something with character — pair a refined sans (e.g., **Geist** for body) with a humanist display face (e.g., **Newsreader** or **Instrument Serif** for warmth at the headline level).

### Signature Design Moves

These are the elements that make Omniscient look like Omniscient and not another generic AI app:

1. **The Pulse** — when actively listening, the entire app subtly breathes. Not a flashing dot. A whole-UI 4-second breath cycle (color shift of 1-2%). Ambient. Peripheral. You feel it without looking at it.

2. **Time-of-day shift** — the background warms up slightly through the day. Cooler in the morning, warmest at golden hour, settling at night. You feel the time without looking at a clock.

3. **The Now Rail** — a thin horizontal strip across the top showing the last 60 seconds of activity. Audio waveform when speech is detected, subtle dots for screen activity, markers for app switches. Always visible, always alive.

4. **Anti-cards** — most information appears WITHOUT containers. Just typography and space. Cards are reserved for things that genuinely need separation (e.g., the chat input, an active recording session). The default is no border, no background.

5. **Empty states with voice** — "I haven't heard anything yet today" instead of "No conversations". The app speaks in first person occasionally — quietly, never overdone.

### Design Principles

1. **Restraint over richness** — when in doubt, remove. The empty space is the design.
2. **Type before color** — solve hierarchy with typography first, color second.
3. **Motion is purposeful** — the breath, the time shift, audio waveforms — all reflect real state. Never decorative animation.
4. **Silence is design** — empty space is the most underused tool. Don't fill it just because you can.
5. **Personality in copy** — never sound like a SaaS product. The app has a quiet voice.

### Anti-References (Things to NEVER Look Like)

- Generic dark-mode SaaS dashboards (Linear copies, Notion copies, Vercel-clone aesthetic)
- Sci-fi command centers with neon glows and cyan accents
- Productivity apps with cards-in-cards everywhere
- AI chat apps with purple gradients (the most overused AI tell of 2024-2025)
- Even Omi itself — we're inspired by Omi's product, not copying its visual language

### Banned Patterns (Hard Rules)

- ❌ Border-left/right accent stripes greater than 1px on cards or list items
- ❌ Gradient text (background-clip: text)
- ❌ Cyan-on-dark or purple-on-dark gradient backgrounds
- ❌ Generic fonts: Inter, Roboto, Space Grotesk, Plus Jakarta Sans, Space Mono, IBM Plex
- ❌ Sparklines used as decoration (only use real data visualization where meaningful)
- ❌ Glassmorphism on every surface (backdrop-filter blur on cards/sidebars/everywhere)
- ❌ Material Symbols icons used decoratively (icons must have a job, not be eye candy)
- ❌ Identical-card grids (the "AI dashboard" anti-pattern)
- ❌ Stats as floating numbers without context ("14 / 53 / 31" means nothing without meaning)

### What "Done" Looks Like

A first-time user who opens the app should:
1. Feel calm immediately — no overwhelm, no information dump
2. Understand the app's purpose within 10 seconds — the empty states teach
3. Notice ONE thing that surprises them — the breath, the time shift, the warmth — something memorable
4. Want to leave it open — not because of constant notifications, but because it feels good to have around

A returning user (10th visit) should:
1. Find what they need in 1 click
2. See clear differences from yesterday — what's new, what's changed
3. Never feel the app is fighting for their attention
